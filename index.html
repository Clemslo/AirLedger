# AirLedger — Privacy Policy *Last updated: July 2026* AirLedger is a Chrome extension for Airbnb hosts. This policy explains what the extension does and does not do with your data. ## Summary AirLedger does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any user data. Everything AirLedger reads and calculates stays on your own device, inside your browser's local storage. There is no server, no account, no analytics, and no tracking of any kind. ## What AirLedger reads When you open your Airbnb host **Reservations** or **multi-calendar** pages (on `www.airbnb.com` or `www.airbnb.fr`) with AirLedger installed, the extension reads reservation data that Airbnb has already loaded into that page for you — check-in/check-out dates, listing name, guest name, confirmation code, reservation status, and payout amount. This is the same data you can already see on the page yourself; AirLedger does not access any additional data, does not log in on your behalf, and does not make any network requests of its own. ## What AirLedger stores The reservation data you scan, along with your saved preferences (average cleaning cost, selected language), is stored using Chrome's built-in `chrome.storage.local` API. This storage is local to your browser profile on your device. AirLedger has no backend server, so this data is never sent anywhere, never leaves your device, and is never seen by the developer. ## What AirLedger does not do - It does not collect personally identifiable information, financial account details, health information, authentication credentials, or browsing history. - It does not use analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting services. - It does not make any network requests to any server, including the developer's own — the extension has no backend. - It does not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties, because no data ever leaves your device. - It does not use your data to determine creditworthiness or for lending purposes. - It does not inject ads or modify Airbnb's pages beyond passively reading data already present. ## Permissions explained - **storage** — to save your scanned reservations and preferences locally on your device, so you don't have to rescan every time you open the popup. - **activeTab** — to check whether the tab you currently have open is an Airbnb host page, and to open the Reservations page for you when you click the corresponding button. - **Access to airbnb.com / airbnb.fr host pages** — required for the extension's single purpose: reading reservation data already displayed on your own Airbnb host pages so it can be counted and summarized. ## Uninstalling Removing the extension from Chrome deletes all locally stored data along with it. There is nothing to delete on any server, because none exists. ## Changes to this policy If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will be revised. Material changes will be reflected in the extension's Chrome Web Store listing. ## Contact For questions about this policy, contact: clement.andry@gmail.com
